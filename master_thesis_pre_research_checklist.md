# Master Thesis Presentation – Pre-Research Checklist

This document defines everything you should complete **before** starting to design slides or a website for your Master thesis defense.

---

## 1. Institutional Requirements (Non‑Negotiable)

Verify officially:

- Exact time limit (minutes)
- Required structure (IMRaD? fixed template?)
- Language (German / English)
- Mandatory slide elements (logo, supervisors, declaration, etc.)
- Is a live demo allowed?
- Q&A format (open discussion, opponent system, structured questioning?)

Failure here can invalidate even a strong presentation.

---

## 2. Evaluation Criteria (Grading Lens)

Clarify how you are graded:

- Scientific depth
- Method correctness
- Practical/industrial relevance
- Critical reflection
- Presentation quality
- Original contribution

This determines how you distribute time between:
- Theory
- Implementation
- Validation
- Discussion

---

## 3. Audience Analysis

Identify:

- Are examiners domain experts?
- Is there a non-specialist member?
- Does your supervisor know every detail?
- Is there an industry partner present?

This affects:
- Technical depth
- Terminology level
- Background explanation time

---

## 4. Core Contribution Distillation (Critical Step)

Write one single page containing:

1. Problem (2 sentences)
2. Research question (1 sentence)
3. Method summary (3 sentences)
4. Top 3 quantitative results (with numbers)
5. Main limitation
6. One strong future direction

If this cannot be written clearly, the presentation will lack focus.

---

## 5. Evidence Audit

Check quality of your material:

- Publication-quality plots
- Clear comparisons
- At least one baseline (if applicable)
- Robustness / sensitivity analysis
- Proper units, scales, labels
- No overcrowded figures

Weak visuals weaken strong research.

---

## 6. Risk Analysis (Technical + Performance)

If using website / 3D / demo:

- Works fully offline
- No external dependencies
- GPU/browser tested
- PDF fallback ready
- Demo recorded as video

Also verify:

- Can you explain each slide without reading?
- Can you stay within time under rehearsal?

---

## 7. Anticipated Committee Questions

Prepare structured answers for:

- Why is this novel?
- Why this method instead of alternative X?
- Where does it fail?
- How generalizable are results?
- What assumptions are critical?
- What would you improve with 6 more months?

Prepare backup slides where necessary.

---

## 8. Story Clarity Test

Within the first 3 minutes, the audience must understand:

- The problem
- Your approach
- Why it matters

If not, restructure before designing.

---

## 9. Design System Decision

Choose one consistent style:

- Classic academic (minimal, Beamer-like)
- Modern web slides (reveal.js / HTML)
- Data-driven technical dashboard
- Visual + 3D enhanced

Do not mix styles mid-development.

---

## 10. Ready-to-Build Checklist

You are ready to start design when:

- Time limit confirmed
- Contributions clearly distilled
- Narrative structure chosen
- Evidence audited
- Audience understood
- Q&A topics prepared
- Technical risks mitigated

Only after this stage should visual design begin.
